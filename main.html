Dr. Kadambini Bose Ganguly

India's first practicing lady doctor

Some important facts about Dr. Kadambini Bose Ganguly life

  • 1861:  She was born in Bihar on 18 July 1861 to a Bengali family, who originated from the Barisal District of pre-partition India (modern day Bangladesh).
  • 1883:   She went onto achieving the Bachelor of Arts from the University of Calcutta.
  • Between 1883 and 1888:   At Calcutta Medical College, Kadambini’s Western medical curriculum included theoretical and practical courses on medicine, surgery and midwifery. She undertook classes and clinical work there and at Calcutta Eden Hospital and at the college’s hospital and dispensary.
  • 1886:   After four years of studying, Kadambini appeared for her final exams.
  • 1888:   Kadambini was appointed to the Lady Dufferin Hospital in Kolkata on a monthly salary of Rs.300.
  • 1889:   Kadambini made history by being among the first six women delegates at the fifth Indian National Congress session.
  • 1890:   The first woman to speak on the platform.
  • 1893:   Few months after arriving in Edinburgh, Kadambini successfully passed her final exams and was awarded the Triple Qualification.
